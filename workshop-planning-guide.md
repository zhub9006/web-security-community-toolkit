## 2. Workshop Planning Checklist

### 4-6 Weeks Before
- [ ] Define learning objectives (1-3 specific outcomes)
- [ ] Confirm venue (capacity, AV, Wi-Fi, accessibility)
- [ ] Set date/time (avoid major Portland events & holidays)
- [ ] Open registration (Eventbrite, Meetup, etc.)
- [ ] Create a workshop outline and slide deck
- [ ] Recruit 1-2 co-facilitators or guest speakers

### 2-3 Weeks Before
- [ ] Send reminder emails to registered attendees
- [ ] Prepare hands-on exercises or simulation scenarios
- [ ] Arrange catering (if not available at venue)
- [ ] Test AV equipment + backup plan
- [ ] Prepare name badges with consent-category opt-in (see Best Practices)
- [ ] Print/privacy-assess workshop materials

### 1 Week Before (Template)
- [ ] Final venue confirmation + room layout check
- [ ] Send "what to bring" and "pre-workshop reading"
- [ ] Share community Slack/Discord invite for the session
- [ ] Prepare contingency plan if fewer attendees show

### Day-of
- [ ] Arrive 45 min early for room setup + tech test
- [ ] Have USB backups + printed materials
- [ ] Set up consent-gated signup sheet (name,email,optional consent categories)
- [ ] Run Privacy 101 warm-up (10 min) before main content
- [ ] Collect post-workshop feedback survey

## 3. Workshop Formats

| Format | Duration | Group Size | Content Focus |
|--------|----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Lunch & Learn** | 45-60 min | 15-40 | Single topic, casual Q&A; low backbone |
| **Deep-Dive Workshop** | 2-3 half-day sessions | 10-25 | Hands-on skills; labs, role-play, simulations |
| **Full-Day Summit** | 8-9 hours | 50-200 | Multiple tracks; keynotes, panels, demos |
| **Monthly Meetup** | 90 min | 10-60 | Lightning talks + open discussion |
| **Privacy Simulation Lab** | 60-90 min | 8-20 | Breach scenario role-play; debrief after |

## 4. Recommended Discussion Topics

1. **Practical Consent Architecture** — Building checkbox-free consent flows
2. **Encryption at Rest & In Transit** — Hands-on TLS/HTTPS and database encryption configs
3. **Zero Trust for Small Communities** — Why "everyone is internal" is a trap
4. **Privacy-First Analytics** — How to run community metrics without PII
5. **De-identification 101** — Tools and pipelines for anonymization
6. **Visual Consent & Readable Policies** — Making privacy docs actually usable
7. **Regulation Deep-Dive** — GDPR/HIPAA/CCPA and what it means for non-profits
8. **Physical Security & Social Engineering** — Defending the human layer

## 5. Portland-Specific Tips

### Transportation
- **TriMet MAX Light Rail** serves all downtown venues (Blue, Red, Green, Orange lines)
- **NE(statement) Streetcar** connects PSU to PSU South campus
- **Bike:** Portland is very bike-friendly; use divvy bike stations near venues
- **Parking:** Limited street parking; prioritize public transit

### Community Resources
- **Portland State University:** Contact the CIS department for collaboration on technical workshops
- **OSU (Oregon State University):** Web security and privacy faculty may be willing to guest lecture
- **Portland Tech:** Non-profit providing free tech education in Portland
- **Tech Liminal:** Coworking space in SE Portland suited for intimate workshops

### Climate & Logistics
- **Rainy season (Nov-Apr):** Choose indoor venues with covered walkways between buildings
- **Summer festivals (July-Aug):** Book well ahead; downtown street closures affect transit routes
- **EV charging:** Available but limited downtown — offer bike shower support for cyclists
