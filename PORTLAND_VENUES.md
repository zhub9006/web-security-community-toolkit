## Pioneer Courthouse Square

- **Address:** 101 SW Broadway, Portland, OR 97201
- **Distance:** ~0.4 km (5-min walk)
- **Type:** Public Square / Open-Air Venue
- **Why it works:** The "living room of Portland" — frequently hosts community events, farmers markets, concerts, and public gatherings. Open, accessible, and in the geographic heart of downtown.
- **Best For:** Outdoor meetups, casual security demos, public awareness campaigns
- **Notes:** Weather-dependent; permit required for organized events

---

## The Old Church Concert Hall

- **Address:** 1422 SW 11th Avenue, Portland, OR 97201
- **Distance:** ~0.4 km (6-min walk)
- **Type:** Historic Events Venue / Concert Hall
- **Why it works:** A beautifully preserved 1882 church with excellent acoustics and a 600-seat main hall. Often used for community events, lectures, and performances.
- **Best For:** Panel discussions, keynote talks, immersive security workshops
- **Notes:** Rental rates available; historic charm adds prestige

---

## Portland Art Museum

- **Address:** 1219 SW Park Avenue, Portland, OR 97205
- **Distance:** ~0.5 km (7-min walk)
- **Type:** Museum / Event Venue
- **Why it works:** One of Portland's premier event venues with private spaces that can accommodate workshops, receptions, and meetups. The building itself is a landmark on Park Avenue.
- **Best For:** Sponsor-branded events, elegant workshops, networking mixers
- **Notes:** Private event rental rates apply; contact the museum's events team for availability

---

## Central Library

- **Address:** 801 SW 10th Avenue, Portland, OR 97205
- **Distance:** ~0.5 km (7-min walk)
- **Type:** Public Library
- **Why it works:** The Portland Public Library offers **free meeting rooms** for community events, which is ideal for nonprofit security workshops. The library is a hub of civic life in downtown Portland.
- **Best For:** Free community workshops, book clubs, open-source working sessions, coding groups
- **Notes:** Request meeting room reservations early; free or very low cost; excellent transit access (adjacent to PSU and MAX line)

---

## Portland State University

- **Address:** 1825 SW Broadway, Portland, OR 97201
- **Distance:** ~0.7 km (9-min walk)
- **Type:** University / Public Venue
- **Why it works:** PSU has extensive event spaces, auditoriums, and classrooms that are sometimes available for community and nonprofit use. The 5th Avenue Metropolitan Public Safety Education Center and various meeting rooms on campus are popular for community events.
- **Best For:** Larger workshops, university-student friendly hackathons, panel discussions
- **Notes:** Check PSU's community events office for room availability and rates

## First Unitarian Church

- **Address:** 1211 SW Main Street, Portland, OR 97205
- **Distance:** ~0.7 km (9-min walk)
- **Type:** Community / Place of Worship (often rents)
- **Why it works:** This historic church is a well-known Portland community venue that frequently hosts civic meetings, workshops, and community gatherings. The space is welcoming and adaptable.
- **Best For:** Inclusive community meetups, film screenings, panel discussions
- **Notes:** Often available for community rental at free or low cost; accessibility-friendly

## The Armory (Portland Center Stage)

- **Address:** 128 NW 11th Avenue, Portland, OR 97209
- **Distance:** ~0.9 km (11-min walk)
- **Type:** Theatre / Events Venue
- **Why it works:** A historic Armory building in the Pearl District with a flexible ground-floor space ideal for workshops, meetups, and presentations. The venue balances community character with professional infrastructure.
- **Best For:** Hands-on security labs, community meetups, film screenings
- **Notes:** Check availability through Portland Center Stage

## World Trade Center

- **Address:** Downtown Portland, Multnomah County, Oregon 97204
- **Distance:** ~0.8 km (10-min walk)
- **Type:** Commercial / Event
- **Why it works:** A commercial center with meeting and event spaces in the heart of downtown. Offers flexible layout options for workshops and presentations.
- **Best For:** Corporate-sponsored meetups, professional workshops, tech talks
- **Notes:** Near Pioneer Courthouse Square and the MAX light rail; easy transit access

---

## Oregon Convention Center

- **Address:** 777 NE Martin Luther King Jr Blvd, Portland, OR 97204
- **Distance:** ~1.5 km (18-min walk / 1 MAX stop)
- **Type:** Conference Centre
- **Why it works:** Portland's primary convention facility with flexible hall space, breakout rooms, and built-in AV infrastructure. Adjacent to the Lloyd Center and MAX Light Rail.
- **Best For:** Large-scale security conferences, multi-track workshops, annual meetups
- **Notes:** Higher rental cost; best for well-funded events; excellent transit access via MAX Yellow/Green lines

---

## Portland Community College – Downtown Center

- **Address:** 722 SW 2nd Avenue, Portland, OR 97204
- **Distance:** ~1.3 km (16-min walk)
- **Type:** Community College / Public Venue
- **Why it works:** PCC offers affordable room and classroom rentals with a strong community and tech-education focus. The Downtown Center is specifically designed for community use.
- **Best For:** Multi-session workshop series, hands-on security labs, affordability-focused events
- **Notes:** Check PCC's community education office for rental rates and availability

---

## Soho House Portland

- **Address:** 1025 SE Pine Street, Portland, OR 97214
- **Distance:** ~2.0 km (25-min walk / short bike ride)
- **Type:** Community / Coworking
- **Why it works:** An exclusive community venue that blends coworking, events, and networking. Has a more intimate, curated feel suitable for small expert groups.
- **Best For:** intimate security circles, expert panels, small-group workshops
- **Notes:** Membership-based venue; may require sponsorship or partnership for community events

## Getting There

All venues listed are within walking distance of downtown Portland's core (SW 1st Ave/Main St area). The **MAX Light Rail** provides additional transit connectivity:

- **Pioneer Courthouse Square** station (Blue/Red/Green/Yellow lines) — central hub
- **Portland State University** station — directly on campus
- **Central Library** station — adjacent to the library
- **Convention Center** station — serves the Oregon Convention Center

## Tips for Event Organizers

1. **Book early** — Popular venues like the Art Museum and PSU fill up quickly
2. **Leverage free options first** — The Library and PCC offer the best cost-effectiveness for nonprofit workshops
3. **Consider transit access** — All venues above are near MAX lines or major bus routes
4. **Accessibility matters** — All listed venues are ADA accessible; confirm specific needs when booking
5. **Food and beverage** — Nearby restaurants (Papa Haydn, Henry Higgins Bagels, Ponte Vecchio) on NW 23rd Ave are great for post-event networking
