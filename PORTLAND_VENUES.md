## Portland State University

- **Address:** 1825 SW Broadway, Portland, OR 97201
- **Distance from Downtown Core:** ~0.8 km walk
- **Type:** University / Public Venue
- **Why it works:** PSU has extensive event spaces, auditoriums, and classrooms that are sometimes available for community and nonprofit use. The 5th Avenue Metropolitan Public Safety Education Center and various meeting rooms on campus are popular for community events.
- **Best For:** Larger workshops, university-student friendly hackathons, panel discussions
- **Notes:** Check PSU's community events office for room availability and rates

## Portland Art Museum

- **Address:** 1219 SW Park Avenue, Portland, OR 97205
- **Distance from Downtown Core:** ~0.5 km walk
- **Type:** Museum / Event Venue
- **Why it works:** One of Portland's premier event venues with private spaces that can accommodate workshops, receptions, and meetups. The building is a landmark on Park Avenue with excellent accessibility.
- **Best For:** Sponsor-branded events, elegant workshops, networking mixers
- **Notes:** Private event rental rates apply; contact the museum's events team for availability

---

## Central Library

- **Address:** 801 SW 10th Avenue, Portland, OR 97205
- **Distance from Downtown Core:** ~1.0 km walk
- **Type:** Public Library
- **Why it works:** The Portland Public Library offers **free meeting rooms** for community events, which is ideal for nonprofit security workshops. The library is a hub of civic life in downtown Portland and is adjacent to PSU on the MAX line.
- **Best For:** Free community workshops, book clubs, open-source working sessions, coding groups
- **Notes:** Request meeting room reservations early; free or very low cost; excellent transit access

## Revolution Hall

- **Address:** 1300 SE Stark Street, Central Eastside / Buckman, Portland, OR 97214
- **Distance from Downtown Core:** ~2.0 km walk (or quick MAX ride)
- **Type:** Events Venue
- **Why it works:** A beloved Portland music and events venue with a flexible main hall that can be configured for meetups, talks, and workshops. Standing-room style ideal for informal gatherings.
- **Best For:** Informal meetups, lightning talks, demo nights, casual hangouts with WiFi
- **Notes:** Check availability through their events team; often used for tech community events

## Oregon Convention Center

- **Address:** 777 NE Martin Luther King Jr Boulevard, Lloyd District, Portland, OR 97204
- **Distance from Downtown Core:** ~2.0 km (adjacent to Lloyd Center, on the MAX Yellow Line)
- **Type:** Convention Centre
- **Why it works:** Large-scale event venue with multiple meeting rooms and ballrooms. The CLT Hub and smaller breakout rooms can host mid-sized workshops. Excellent transit connectivity.
- **Best For:** Larger conferences, multi-track workshops, vendor-sponsored meetups
- **Notes:** Contact the events team for room rental rates; book well in advance for large events

## Portland Center Stage at The Armory

- **Address:** 128 NW 11th Avenue, The Brewery Blocks, Pearl District, Portland, OR 97209
- **Distance from Downtown Core:** ~1.2 km walk
- **Type:** Theatre / Event Venue
- **Why it works:** A professional theatre venue in the heart of the Pearl District with flexible event configurations. The building's historic character (former armory) provides a unique backdrop for security workshops.

- **Notes:** Contact Portland Center Stage for private event rentals

## Getting There

All venues listed are within walking distance or a short MAX ride from downtown Portland's core (SW 1st Ave / Main St area). The **MAX Light Rail** provides additional connectivity:

- **Pioneer Courthouse Square** station (Blue/Red/Green/Yellow lines) — central hub
- **PSU South** station — directly on campus
- **Central Library** station — adjacent to the library
- **Lloyd Center** station — near Oregon Convention Center
- **Revolution Hall / 13th Ave** — nearby for Buckman venues

## Tips for Event Organizers

1. **Book early** — Popular venues like the Art Museum and PSU fill up quickly
2. **Leverage free options first** — The Library and PCC offer the best cost-effectiveness for nonprofit workshops
3. **Consider transit access** — All venues above are near MAX lines or major bus routes
4. **Accessibility matters** — All listed venues are ADA accessible; confirm specific needs when booking
5. **Food and beverage** — Nearby restaurants on NW 23rd Ave (Papa Haydn, Henry Higgins Bagels) are great for post-event networking
